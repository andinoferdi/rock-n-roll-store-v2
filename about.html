Every person on our floor plays an instrument. Some play multiple. Before you get a recommendation here, whoever helps you has probably owned, gigged, broken, and repaired something similar.

That's not a marketing line. It's a hiring policy. We don't bring on floor staff who can't tell you why a tube amp distorts differently from a solid-state, or why nut width matters on a fingerpicking guitar.

If we wouldn't play it ourselves, it doesn't go on the floor. That standard has cost us some vendor deals over the years. We're fine with that.

Our Story

Started With a Strat
and a Bad Landlord

Marcus Webb and Yuki Tanaka met at a session in Bushwick in 2007. Marcus played lead guitar in three bands simultaneously. Yuki built her own effects pedals and toured with a jazz quartet.

Neither of them planned to open a store. But every time they needed gear — something specific, something that wasn't mass-produced garbage — they ended up driving to three different shops and still leaving empty-handed or oversold something they didn't need.

In 2009, when a small storefront on Nostrand Avenue became available, they took it on a handshake and a shared credit card. The first month's inventory was 40 guitars, two amps, and a wall of pedals Yuki had personally tested over the previous year.

Fifteen years later: same neighborhood, 8,000+ products, 18 staff, and still the same policy — if it's on the floor, someone here has played it.

What We Stand For

Four things
we won't negotiate.

Most stores say they care about quality. We tried to write down what that actually means in practice — the decisions it forces us to make, and the ones it makes for us.

01
No salesperson who can't play.

It sounds like a small thing. It isn't. When someone walks in asking about a guitar for their first year of serious practice, the difference between a player giving advice and a salesperson giving advice is measured in hundreds of dollars and months of frustration. Every floor hire plays at least one instrument at a gigging level. That's the bar. It's non-negotiable.

02
If we won't gig it, we won't sell it.

Every brand we carry has been played by at least two staff members before it goes on the floor. Some great-looking brands have never made it in because they didn't hold up under real use. That filter costs us margin. It costs customers nothing.

03
Setup is part of the sale.

Every guitar we ship gets a setup before it leaves. Intonation checked, action adjusted, neck relief set. Not a sticker that says "professionally inspected." Actual work by someone who knows what they're doing.

04
Trade-ins are priced honestly.

We don't lowball instruments and flip them for double. We price what they're worth, explain how we got there, and let the seller decide. Repeat business matters more to us than margin on a single transaction.
